- Note: While this is great for a quick visual check with .show(), it's not very helpful for massive datasets with millions of rows (you can't scroll through a million "falses").


Better approach:
from pyspark.sql.functions import col, count, when

# This counts how many nulls exist per column
df.select([count(when(isnull(c), c)).alias(c) for c in df.columns]).show()
